Balloon Angioplasty Simulation

Introduction

Cardiovascular diseases often involve arterial narrowing caused by plaque buildup, restricting blood flow and increasing the risk of heart attack or stroke. Balloon Angioplasty is a common medical intervention that restores arterial diameter by expanding a stent radially inside the narrowed segment. This project models the mechanical response of the artery, plaque, and stent during expansion using finite element analysis.

Methods

  • Created artery–plaque–stent geometry.
  • Assigned hyperelastic material properties to arterial tissue and stiff properties to plaque.
  • Applied fixed boundary constraints to arterial ends.
  • Simulated radial stent expansion to replicate Balloon Angioplasty.
  • Used refined mesh at plaque–artery interface for accurate stress capture.

Results

  • Stent expansion successfully increased lumen diameter.
  • Stress concentrations appeared at the plaque–artery interface.
  • Non-uniform deformation observed due to plaque stiffness.
  • Restored cross-sectional area indicated successful angioplasty.

Discussion / Evaluation

The simulation captured realistic deformation patterns and stress distributions that align with clinical expectations. Future work includes patient-specific geometries, pulsatile flow loading, and viscoelastic arterial models.
